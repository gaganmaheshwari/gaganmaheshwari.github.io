    An interpretation of ambiguity that is not ambiguous

    02 Jul 2016 in Leadership     3.02 minutes read

    What does ambiguity and ambiguous situations really mean?

    Ambiguity is just the opposite of clarity. Ambiguity is a state of vagueness, a state of mind when it is confusing to make a choice between two or more competing alternatives in a decisive manner and pursue the choice with absolute commitment.

    Eternality of ambiguity

    Life is a game of choices which means that ambiguity cannot be avoided. It is essential to understand that ambiguity exists in the mind of individuals. Since all of us are unique in our own ways, a situation that may be ambiguous for one could be unambiguous for another.

    Examples of ambiguous situations

    It’s always easier to comprehend a subject if there are appropriate examples. It is, however, difficult to come up with a universally agreeable and acceptable set of ambiguous situation since all of us happen to have our own unique thought process, perceptions, education, exposure and experiences in life.

    So, with that said, you can take the examples below with a grain of salt as they may or may not seem ambiguous in your opinion.

    Examples of ambiguious and confusing situations in personal life

    • Choice of career to pursue
    • Choice of location to live
    • Choice of specific assets to buy
    • Choosing between work-life balance vs professional growth

    Examples of ambiguious and confusing situations in professional and business world

    • Choice of starting business by yourself or with partners
    • Choosing the right marketing budget and marketing mediums
    • Deciding product features to release
    • Deciding which bugs to resolve first
    • Deciding response to complicated situations
    • Deciding the appropriate way to communicate discontentment
    • Deciding whether to continue to invest in a not-so-successful product

    Should you dread ambiguity?

    In my opinion, ambiguity is not a bad thing. Ambiguity in situations and in making decisions is quite commonplace for smart people. Smart people are the ones with the options anyways. The idea is simple - more you know, more ideas you will have, more possibilities you can envision, more choices you will generate, more data points you can fathom to support your choices and hence, more the ambiguity that you have to deal with.

    Does that strengthen your belief in the old adage that “ignorance is bliss”?

    Understanding the core of ambiguity

    Making a choice is usually not the big deal. Most of the times, ambiguity arises from the pressure of making the right choice, the thought of living with that choice, and being responsible and accountable for that choice. Having this understanding and realization by itself goes a long way in effectively tackling and managing ambiguity.

    In my opinion, having multiple answers, choices, solutions and options to a given situation or problem, which may result in ambiguity, is the characteristic of a smart mind. However, staying confused or having a decision-making paralysis is a characteristic of the weak.

    Conclusion

    Ambiguity is the lack of clarity and certainty in the ability to make a choice. The impending confusion of choices and being accountability for making the choice is at the core of any ambiguous situation.

    Effectively dealing with ambiguous situations is what differentiates the leaders from the followers.

    So, do not fear or avoid ambiguity but rather manage it. It is usually the smart minds which can think in terms of choices, make informed choices, re-calibrate those choices, be agile in making alternate choices and who constantly celebrate those choices. Dealing with ambiguity is just a mindset!

    The good news is that it is possible to train and educate ourselves to effectively manage and respond in ambiguous situations. In the next post, I am going to be covering strategies and techniques to effectively manage ambiguity.
